Bus tickets (Pulapromet)

How to pay €0.90–2

€0.90 with a contactless bank card (tap on board) · €1.50 if bought at a Tisak kiosk or shop.pulapromet.hr · €2.00 from the driver in cash. One ticket = 1 hour, one direction.

Cheapest & easiest: just tap a contactless card as you board. Keep small coins as backup.
Key connections

Airport → hotel

~8 km, 15–20 min. Easiest for an evening arrival: Bolt or taxi ~€15–20 straight to Carlijeva 43. (Airport shuttle is €6 but only to the centre, then a transfer.)

To the beaches

Line 3 / 2 → Verudela (Ambrela, Hawaii, Zlatne Stijene). Line 1 → Stoja · Line 4 / 6 → Valkane. Or just walk the Lungomare (~25–30 min).

Day-trip buses (from the main terminal)

Rovinj: intercity bus ~€5, 30–40 min. Brijuni: city bus #21 → Fažana, then park boat. Kamenjak: bus #28 (sometimes #26) → Premantura.

Bus terminal

Late nights

City buses thin out after ~23:00. After the Arena film, grab a Bolt/taxi back to Veruda (~€8–10) — the app works well in Pula.

Beaches & swimming

Closest-to-you first · sea ~25 °C

Istrian beaches are pebbly / rocky, not sandy. Pack water shoes (watch for sea urchins), water & snacks — beach kiosks are pricey.

Eat & drink on a budget

Istrian flavours without the tourist mark-up

Taste of Istria — order these

Fuži / pljukanci (hand-made pasta) with truffle or game sauce · maneštra (bean & veg stew) · Istrian pršut (prosciutto) & cheese · fresh grilled fish & black cuttlefish risotto · fritule (little doughnuts). Wash down with Malvazija (white) or Teran (red) — ~€3–4 a glass.

Budget hacks: look for the midday “gablec” workers’ lunch menu · picnic from the market & a bakery · tap water is free & safe (ask for it) · sit one street back from the Forum for lower prices.

Pula Film Festival

73rd edition · 9–16 July 2026 · you’re here for it!

The unmissable bit
A film in the Roman Arena

Open-air cinema inside a 2,000-year-old amphitheatre — one of only two in the world still used this way. Deeply romantic and only €6 a ticket.

Tickets €6 · Arena

Buy online (official festival site), at the Giardini Info Point, or the kiosk in front of the Arena. On-site sales run 9:00–13:00 & 18:00–24:00. Popular nights sell out — buy earlier in the day.

Timing

Arena screenings start after dusk (~21:30). Arrive ~30 min early for good seats and bring a light layer. Films are Croatian premieres (often English-subtitled) & international titles — check the day’s programme at the Info Point.

Other venues

150+ screenings across town — Valli Cinema, Istrian National Theatre, and open-air Ambrela. Some outdoor programmes are free.

Perfect romantic evening: early dinner in the old town → stroll the lit-up Forum → film under the stars.

Essentials

Money

Croatia uses the Euro (€). Cards accepted widely; carry some cash for buses, markets & small konobas. Use bank ATMs — decline “conversion”, and avoid standalone Euronet machines (poor rates).

Phone & internet

Croatia is in the EU (roam-free for EU SIMs). Swiss SIMs are not EU — data can be pricey, so consider a cheap Croatia/EU eSIM or check your plan. Free Wi-Fi in most cafés & the hotel.

Power

Sockets are Type C / F, 230 V (standard EU). Coming from Switzerland? Your Swiss plugs need a small adapter; most USB chargers (Europlug) fit fine.

Water & sun

Tap water is safe & tastes fine — refill a bottle. July sun is strong: SPF, hat, midday shade, and drink plenty.

Tipping

Not obligatory. Round up or leave ~10% for good service in restaurants.

Language

Croatian — but this is Istria, so Italian is widely spoken and English is common in tourism. A few Croatian words go a long way (see the Croatian tab).
